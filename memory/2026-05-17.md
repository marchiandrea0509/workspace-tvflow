## Mandatory output format for future deep analyses

Do not collapse chat replies into only “Chart Context Read + Final Verdict”. Future deep-analysis chat summaries and saved reports must include all required sections in order:

1. Chart Context Read
2. Market State
3. Key Levels
4. Trade Quality
5. Option A — BEST QUALITY
6. Option B — BEST FILL PROBABILITY
7. Orderability
8. Risk Sizing
9. Trade Plan Tickets
10. Rejection Audit if anything is rejected / conditional / WAIT / NO_TRADE / NOT_PLACEABLE_YET
11. Final Verdict

For every valid option, including `VALID + PLACEABLE_CONDITIONAL_ONLY`, print a full ticket table. Conditional means “wait for the stated trigger/rejection before placing”, not “omit the ticket”. Required ticket columns:

`leg | order type | entry | notional $ | quantity | SL | loss at SL $ | TP/profit $ | R:R | trigger`

Each valid-option ticket row must include its own entry, quantity, SL, TP, loss at SL, profit at TP, R:R, and explicit trigger condition. For conditional tickets, write trigger text directly in the row, e.g. “Only after 1H rejection from 137.35”, “Only after failed reclaim of 140.50”, “Only after 4H rejection below 143.09”, or “Only after breakdown/retest below 130.26”.

Risk sizing must always be calculated for every valid option:
- risk per leg
- total risk
- notional size
- quantity
- estimated margin
- suggested leverage
- blended entry if all legs fill
- all-filled R:R

Use `$100` max planned risk per option unless user specifies otherwise. A and B are alternatives, not simultaneous. If exact quantity cannot be calculated, provide an approximate ticket from current symbol price and entry/SL distance and mark approximate; do not omit the ticket.

If the chat surface forces a shorter summary and a valid ticket is omitted, explicitly say: “Ticket table omitted from chat, full ticket available in saved report.” Prefer printing the ticket in chat.

## Option A / Option B construction rule

Andrea clarified that Option A and Option B must be different trade paths, not merely two versions of the same ladder.

Option A — BEST QUALITY:
- Use the cleanest 4H parent-structure setup.
- For pullback/rally trades, use the visually meaningful 4H parent swing, not the latest tiny local impulse.
- Normally should have better R:R, cleaner invalidation, and cleaner structure.

Option B — BEST FILL PROBABILITY:
- Do **not** force Option B to be a shallow pullback/rally ladder.
- It should be the best higher-probability alternative path.
- It may be a shallower pullback/rally ladder if structurally valid, OR a BREAKOUT/BREAKDOWN stop-entry if price is already close to major resistance/support and may continue without a pullback.

Decision rules:
- Bearish setup near major support: do not default Option B to shallow sell-rally; test a BREAKDOWN stop-entry below major support as preferred fill-probability alternative. Use shallow sell-rally only if it clearly has better structure/R:R than the breakdown trigger.
- Bullish setup near major resistance: do not default Option B to shallow dip-ladder; test a BREAKOUT stop-entry above major resistance as preferred fill-probability alternative. Use shallow dip-ladder only if it clearly has better structure/R:R than the breakout trigger.

Every analysis must state:
- Option A setup path: parent pullback/rally OR other
- Option B setup path: shallow pullback/rally OR breakout/breakdown
- why Option B was chosen as the fill-probability alternative
- why shallow ladder was chosen/rejected
- why breakout/breakdown stop was chosen/rejected
