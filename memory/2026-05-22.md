## JDUSDT SL investigation
- Andrea asked why the JDUSDT.P live 7x long ladder lost about `160 USDT` despite planned SL `31.35` and planned risk `~100 USDT`.
- Read-only Bitget account export for `JDUSDT` since 2026-05-19 found the original entry orders all filled: L1 `28.09 @ 32.24`, L2 `55.56 @ 31.98`, L3 `121.21 @ 31.68`, each with preset SL `31.35`.
- Stop exits were Bitget system `loss_market` orders created around `2026-05-21T13:33:29Z`, not missing/cancelled SLs. Aggregate position was `204.86 JD`, blended entry `31.83815`, blended exit `31.04958`.
- Planned SL risk at `31.35` was `~100.00 USDT`; actual price loss was `~161.55 USDT`; close fees were `~2.67 USDT`; net including close fees was `~164.22 USDT`. Extra price slippage versus the SL level was `~61.54 USDT`.
- Public Bitget market data around the stop showed 13:33 UTC 1m candle low `30.93` and exact `13:33:29.xxx` public trade volume `257.74 JD` with VWAP `31.07223`; the `204.86 JD` stop size was about `79.5%` of that second's public traded volume.
- Root cause: Bitget preset SL executed as market stop into a thin JDUSDT order book during a fast sell sweep. The SL trigger existed, but execution was not guaranteed at `31.35`.
- Investigation report/export saved under `reports/investigations/jd_sl_20260522/`: `JDUSDT_stop_loss_investigation.md`, `JDUSDT_stop_loss_investigation_export.zip`, raw account history, account fill distribution CSV, public 1m candles, and public trades window.
- Durable rule: future live TradFi-perp ladders need a live liquidity/slippage gate; planned risk must include stop-market slippage on thin symbols. If depth/recent-volume cannot absorb the full stop size within tolerance, reduce size/leverage, skip large L3, or avoid the symbol.
